06 · Common questions

The "will my agent accept this?" list.

If you don't see your question, write to us. A human reads everything.

Will my exported PDF look like a real screenplay?

Yes. Margins, font, page numbers, MORE/CONTINUED handling, title page — all to industry standard. Open one in Final Draft and you won't see a difference, except your agent will get to your story faster.

Can I import an existing Final Draft file?

Drop in a .fdx, a Fountain file, or a clean PDF. Slima parses the scene structure, character list, and beat hints automatically.

Does the AI write scenes for me?

Only when asked. The default mentor critiques, suggests within structure, and stays in your characters' voices. You can ask it to draft a scene if you're stuck — but you choose what makes the page.

What about TV — episodes, seasons, writers' rooms?

Episodes share a series Bible. The corkboard supports multi-episode arcs. Writers' rooms can collaborate on a single document with comment threads — no "v3-final-FINAL_v2" anywhere in sight.

How is this different from Final Draft?

Final Draft is excellent at formatting and weak everywhere else. Slima brings the corkboard, beat sheet, character bible, AI mentor, and exports together — for screenwriters specifically, in 2026, not 2003.

Is there a free tier?

The free trial gives you the full Script Studio for two weeks. After that, Starter is $9/month — the same price as a streaming subscription, for the studio that helps you make the next show on it.
