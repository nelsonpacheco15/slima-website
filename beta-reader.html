06 · Common questions

The "but is it any good?" list.

If you don't see your question, write to us. A human reads everything.

Are the readers actually reading my whole book?

Yes. The full text — up to 100K words per report — is read end to end. Each reader holds the entire manuscript in context while writing their notes, so when David flags a chapter-six contradiction with chapter one, that's a real cross-chapter call.

Can I bring my own readers?

You can. The "Build your own" persona lets you define taste, age, reading history, pet peeves, and a voice sample. The reader stays consistent across reports for as long as you keep them.

What if my readers disagree?

That's a feature. The report flags every disagreement explicitly so you can see the split and make a real authorial choice — not average everyone into mush.

Is this a replacement for a human editor?

No. It's a replacement for the friend who said they'd read your draft and then didn't. For final edits, hire a person — and bring the Slima report with you. It saves billable hours.

Can I run the same panel on a revision?

Yes — re-running the same panel on a revised draft is the most valuable thing you can do. The report shows the deltas: what improved, what got worse, what you accidentally broke fixing something else.

How is this different from Beta readers I'd find online?

Online betas are humans, which is wonderful and slow. Slima is structured, scored, and arrives in roughly a day. Most novelists use both — Slima for early passes, humans for the final read.
